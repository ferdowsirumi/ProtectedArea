Protected Areas Around the World

A protected area is a clearly defined geographical space, recognised, dedicated and managed, through legal or other effective means, to achieve the long term conservation of nature with associated ecosystem services and cultural values.
-- (IUCN Definition 2008)

Protected Areas includes:

Natural Parks
Wilderness areas
Community conserved areas
Natural reserves and so on.

Importance of Protected Areas

Protected areas are incresingly recoznized for their role in adapting and mitigating climate change. They are mainstay oof biodiversity conservation. These areas also contributing to peoples lively hood by providing food, water supply and saves us from natural disasters.

Conserve 15% of Carbon

Global network of Protected Areas conserve 15% of terrestrial carbon.

Biodiversity Conservation

Protected Areas plays major role in bio-diversity conservation around the world.

Provides Food, Water and Shelter

At local level they provide clean water supply, food, medicines as well as shelter from natural disasters.
